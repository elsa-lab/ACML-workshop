The 2nd Workshop on Machine Learning for Mobile Robot Vision and Control (MRVC-2022)

Description

This is the second Workshop on Machine Learning for Mobile Robot Vision and Control (MRVC). The first MRVC workshop was held in conjunction with the 13th Asian Conference on Machine Learning (ACML 2021). MRVC-2021 was very successful. It featured three keynote speeches, several paper presentations, and had attracted more than fifty attendees to join the event.

Time: Half-Day Workshop

Theme

Workshop Theme

In recent years, the advances in machine learning for vision and control applications support the increasing demands for mobile robots. Such demands surge especially in the past two years, arguably due to the spread of COVID-19. While mobile robots are typically expected to work in controlled environments (e.g., supply chain automation at factories), more challenging unconstrained situations (e.g., cleaning, sanitizing, etc.) have also begun attracting attention, which in turn causes automation and safety of mobile robots to become a serious concern.

To enable mobile robots to meet such demands, equipping them with satisfactory vision and control capabilities is necessary and has become the key focus of relevant robotic research endeavors. Many sophisticated robotics, machine learning, and computer vision approaches have been developed to meet this aim. However, these advances have not yet been properly translated to significant progress in practical mobile robot applications due to the insufficiency of effective data samples from the real world, leading to unsatisfactory performance and safety concerns of mobile robots during deployment.

Moreover, effectively collecting data and efficiently utilizing them for training vision and control models, especially in unconstrained outdoor environments, have further raised a number of fundamental challenges for mobile robot applications. These challenges include several open but crucial issues, such as multimodal sensing, privacy issues, human activity recognition and prediction, as well as the constraints on batteries, computing capabilities, and limited field of view.

To better understand the aforementioned issues and improve the current solutions, this MRVC workshop presents a timely opportunity to bring together researchers in robotics, computer vision, and machine learning communities together to discuss the unique challenges and opportunities for mobile robots.

Discussion

Discussion of the Scope of the Workshop

In this workshop, we aim to bring together the machine learning, computer vision, and robotics research communities. The workshop will mainly focus on the increasingly important role of learning in mobile robots, and will solicit contributions at the intersection of mobile robotics and the areas shown above in both theory and applications. Specific topics of interest will include but not limited to:

  • Machine learning for mobile robot control
    • Planning in unknown environments or with limited data
    • Reinforcement learning and imitation learning for robot navigation and exploration
    • Cooperative multi-agent control, learning to cooperate and communicate, multi-agent planning
    • Probabilistic learning and representation of uncertainty in robotics
    • Meta learning
  • Machine learning for unconstrained and real environments
    • Domain adaptation
    • Federated learning
    • Privacy-preserving ML
    • Synthetic dataset curation, simulation & training framework
  • Computer vision for mobile robots
    • Vision based localization and mapping (Visual Odometry, SLAM)
    • Low-level vision
    • Mid-level representation for robot learning (e.g., semantic segmentation, optical flow, and depth estimation)
    • Trajectory forecasting
    • Human activity recognition and prediction
    • Multimodal perception (e.g., vision, language, text, audio, etc.), sensor fusion
    • Embodied and egocentric vision techniques
  • Other applications of learning in robot manipulation, navigation, driving, flight, and other areas of robotics

We will solicit both novel unpublished and accepted works in a short paper format (i.e., up to four pages). Works in progress are also welcome to submit to this workshop as extended abstracts (i.e., up to two pages). We will also invite some interesting but under-represented works published in other venues to help them improve their presence. Either way, we will assign at least two experienced reviewers for every paper to give constructive feedback.

The workshop will be held in a hybrid format and will feature single-track presentations and a poster session for the accepted papers.

Plans to Encourage Participation

The MRVC workshop views diversity and inclusion seriously. We will design the workshop to be inclusive for diverse attendees. Although the workshop will be held at the time period allocated by CoRL, we will actively utilize text-based communications among participants (e.g., chat functions in Zoom and Gather.Town, or slack/discord channels created for the workshop) so that participants whose native language is not English can use translation tools to participate in the discussion without difficulty, anytime in synchronized and asynchronized fashions. In addition, the workshop will be video recorded, which will be put on YouTube or other open streaming platforms to promote knowledge sharing for the public. The contents (i.e., papers, posters, and videos) will be put on the workshop website. The potential attendees who are unable to attend the workshop will still have access to the workshop contents afterwards.

Physical Resources

MRVC-2021 had attracted more than 50 attendees to participate in the event. In this year, MRVC-2022 targets at attracting more than a hundred participants.

Physical resource needed

  • Poster boards: 15 poster boards are required on site for discussion and presentation.
  • Conference room: For accommodating a hundred participants.

Hybrid Format

The workshop will be held in hybrid format. The main workshop will be held in person. The primary organizer(s) will attend the workshop in-person.

For the virtual workshop, we will select between Zoom / YouTube (for keynote and oral presentations), and Gatherly / Gather.Town (oral and/or poster presentations). In addition, we will also consider having asynchronized text-based discussions (e.g., Slack or Discord) as our platform to solve the time difference issue for worldwide virtual participants. These services have great interfaces for desktops, smartphones, and tablets, and have been commonly used for virtual conferences and our daily online discussions. We believe that using them in combination will be ideal to make the workshop easily accessible to diverse attendees.

Accepted papers and posters will also be made available on our workshop website. The workshop presentations will be video recorded and made public during and/or after the workshop.
